We get into both, plus the OpenAI Plus pricing cut on Friday — a churn defense, not a price war.

## Key takeaways

- Anthropic's Opus 5 launch is the noise. The Sonnet 5 price drop is the signal — they expect inference cost to keep falling and they want the volume tier locked in before OpenAI gets there.
- Spotify's 28% podcast ad growth is a programmatic-buying story, not a content story. The 4.2B API requests in March is the metric that explains the quarter.
- Spotify wrote down $200M on an exclusive deal this quarter and buried it in OpEx. The exclusive-deal era is dead — they just won't say it on the call.
- Apple is two quarters away from copying the Spotify programmatic playbook. The "Spotify wins audio" story has a short shelf life.
- OpenAI's Plus pricing cut is churn defense, not growth offense. $80M/month off the topline is the cost of holding the install base.
